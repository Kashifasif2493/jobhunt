Top Remote Companies Hiring Worldwide in 2026

📅 July 2026 ⏱️ 9 min read ✍️ WorldJobShunt Team

One of the biggest mistakes remote job seekers make is applying to random listings without knowing which companies are actually committed to remote work — not just tolerating it temporarily, but genuinely built around it.

There is a big difference between a company that allows some employees to work from home and a company that operates fully remotely, has built its culture around distributed teams, and actively hires talent from anywhere in the world. The second type of company is where remote workers thrive — and those are the ones worth targeting in your job search.

In this guide, we have put together a list of the top companies actively hiring remote workers worldwide in 2026 — what they do, what kinds of roles they hire for, and what makes them worth applying to.

💡 Pro tip: When applying to remote companies, always check their careers page directly — not just job boards. Many remote-first companies post roles on their own website first, and those listings often get fewer applicants than the same role posted on LinkedIn or Indeed.

Fully Remote Companies Worth Targeting

Company 01

Automattic

Automattic is the company behind WordPress.com, WooCommerce, Tumblr, and several other major web products. They have been fully remote since their founding and employ over 1,900 people across more than 95 countries. They hire across engineering, design, support, marketing, and business roles — and they are well known for their thoughtful hiring process and strong remote culture. Pay is globally competitive and they offer generous benefits including a home office allowance.

Fully Remote 95+ Countries Tech & Support
Company 02

GitLab

GitLab is one of the most well-known remote-first companies in the world and a genuine pioneer of distributed work. Their entire company — over 2,000 people — works remotely across more than 60 countries. They are known for radical transparency, publishing their entire company handbook publicly online. GitLab hires heavily in engineering, product, sales, marketing, and people operations. They also pay at or above market rates regardless of location, which makes them particularly attractive for candidates based outside the US or Europe.

Fully Remote 60+ Countries Global Pay
Company 03

Shopify

Shopify, the e-commerce platform used by millions of businesses worldwide, declared itself a "digital by default" company and has maintained a primarily remote workforce. They hire across engineering, data, design, merchant support, and business development. Shopify is known for competitive compensation, strong benefits, and a culture that genuinely invests in employee development. They are particularly active in hiring from Canada, Europe, and increasingly from Asia and Latin America.

Remote First Multiple Roles Strong Benefits
Company 04

Zapier

Zapier is an automation platform that connects apps and workflows — and they have been fully remote since day one. With over 700 employees spread across the world, they are proof that a tech company can scale successfully without offices. Zapier hires in engineering, product, design, customer support, content, and marketing. They are known for a strong writing culture — their job applications often involve written assessments, so polish your communication skills before applying.

Fully Remote Writing Culture All Roles
Company 05

Basecamp

Basecamp makes project management software and has been a vocal advocate for remote work for over two decades — their founders wrote the book on it, literally. They are a smaller company that hires selectively, but when they do have openings they are worth applying for. Basecamp is known for reasonable working hours, no meetings culture, and a strong emphasis on written communication. They hire primarily in product, engineering, and customer support.

Fully Remote Small Team Async Culture

Company 06

HubSpot

HubSpot, the marketing and CRM platform, shifted to a hybrid and remote model and has significantly expanded its global remote hiring. They hire across sales, marketing, engineering, customer success, and finance roles. HubSpot is particularly well known for its sales roles, which are available remotely in multiple regions. They offer strong career development programmes, a generous benefits package, and a culture that emphasises employee wellbeing and growth.

Remote Friendly Sales & Marketing Career Growth
Company 07

Toptal

Toptal is a network that connects top freelance developers, designers, finance experts, and project managers with companies worldwide. If you are accepted into the Toptal network — which involves a rigorous screening process — you get access to consistent, well-paid remote project work with companies that include Fortune 500 clients. It is not a traditional employment relationship, but for senior freelancers, it is one of the most valuable remote income sources available.

Freelance Network Senior Level High Pay
Company 08

Twilio

Twilio is a cloud communications platform that powers messaging, voice, and video features for thousands of apps worldwide. They operate a distributed team model with remote roles available in multiple regions. Twilio hires heavily in engineering, product, developer relations, sales, and customer success. They are known for a strong engineering culture and a clear commitment to developer education and community building.

Remote Roles Engineering Focus Multiple Regions
Company 09

Cloudflare

Cloudflare provides internet security and performance services to millions of websites. They have significantly expanded their remote hiring and now have distributed teams across the Americas, Europe, and Asia Pacific. Cloudflare hires in engineering, security, sales, legal, and operations. They are known for competitive compensation and a strong engineering culture, and they regularly post remote roles open to candidates across multiple countries simultaneously.

Remote Roles Global Hiring Tech & Security
Company 10

Buffer

Buffer is a social media management tool and one of the most transparent remote companies in existence — they publish their salaries, equity, and revenue figures publicly. They are a small team of around 80 people working fully remotely across multiple time zones. Buffer hires infrequently but when they do, their roles attract strong interest. They are particularly known for their async-first culture and their emphasis on work-life balance.

Fully Remote Transparent Culture Async First

How to Get Hired by a Remote Company

Applying to remote companies is different from applying to traditional employers. The competition is global — you are not competing with people in your city, you are competing with candidates from everywhere. That raises the bar, but it also means the opportunity is genuinely open to anyone with the right skills regardless of where they live.

Research the Company Culture

Remote-first companies care deeply about cultural fit because they cannot rely on in-person interactions to build team cohesion. Read their blog, their handbook if they publish one, their Glassdoor reviews, and anything their employees have written publicly. Show in your application that you understand how they work — not just what they do.

Demonstrate Async Communication Skills

Most fully remote companies operate asynchronously — meaning people work at different times and communicate primarily through written messages rather than meetings. Your application, cover letter, and any written assessments are evidence of your async communication ability. Write clearly, concisely, and with the level of detail that makes your message easy to act on without follow-up questions.

Show Self-Direction

Remote employers are not looking for people who need to be managed closely. They want people who can identify what needs to be done, prioritise independently, and follow through without constant check-ins. Your application should give evidence of this — specific examples of projects you drove independently, problems you solved without being asked, and outcomes you achieved without supervision.

💡 Cover letter tip for remote roles: Always mention your remote work experience or setup in your cover letter — even if the job posting does not ask for it. Mention your home office setup, your experience with async tools like Slack, Notion, or Jira, and any previous remote work you have done. It signals that you are prepared for the reality of distributed work.

Where to Find Remote Job Listings for These Companies

The best place to find openings at remote-first companies is their own careers pages — bookmarked and checked regularly. Most of the companies on this list also post on We Work Remotely, Remote.co, and LinkedIn with remote filters applied. WorldJobShunt aggregates listings from verified sources daily and covers many of these employers, making it a useful starting point for your daily check.

Final Thoughts

The companies on this list are not just offering remote work as a perk — they have built their entire operations around distributed teams. Working for one of them means joining a culture that genuinely understands remote work, invests in the tools and processes that make it sustainable, and values output over presence.

The competition for these roles is real. But so is the opportunity. A strong CV, a tailored cover letter, and a genuine understanding of remote work culture will take you a long way toward landing a role with one of the best remote employers in the world.
