Best Websites to Find Remote Jobs in 2026

Published May 2026  ·  6 min read

Finding a remote job requires knowing where to look. Not all job boards are created equal — some are filled with outdated listings, spam, or roles that are not actually remote. Others are laser-focused on genuine remote opportunities with reputable companies.

Here are the best websites to find remote jobs in 2026, based on listing quality, update frequency, and the types of roles available.

Top Remote Job Boards

1

WorldJobShunt

Our own platform aggregates thousands of remote and worldwide job listings daily from verified sources. Search by category, country, and keyword to find exactly what you are looking for — all in one place, updated automatically.

Free · Updated Daily
2

LinkedIn

LinkedIn remains the most powerful professional networking platform for job seekers. The job board has millions of listings, including a dedicated remote filter. The real advantage is the ability to connect with recruiters and hiring managers directly.

Free · Networking + Jobs
3

We Work Remotely

One of the oldest and most trusted remote-only job boards. Companies like GitHub, Basecamp, and Buffer regularly post on here. The listings are curated and high quality, with a focus on tech, design, and marketing roles.

Remote Only · Tech Focus
4

Remote.co

Remote.co features hand-screened remote job listings across many industries including customer service, writing, HR, and education — not just tech. It also has helpful resources for remote workers and employers.

Remote Only · All Industries
5

Upwork

The world's largest freelance marketplace. Ideal for finding contract and project-based remote work in writing, design, development, marketing, and more. Building a strong Upwork profile takes time but can lead to a full-time remote income.

Freelance · All Skills
6

Indeed

Indeed is one of the largest general job boards in the world. Use the remote filter when searching. The volume of listings is enormous — which means there is something for almost every skill level and industry.

Free · Worldwide
7

Glassdoor

Glassdoor combines job listings with company reviews and salary data — making it one of the most useful platforms for researching employers before applying. The remote filter helps narrow down location-flexible roles.

Free · Reviews + Salaries

Tips for Using Job Boards Effectively

  • Set up job alerts on multiple platforms so new listings come to your inbox
  • Apply within the first 24-48 hours of a listing going live — early applicants get more attention
  • Tailor your CV and cover letter for each role — do not use the same generic application everywhere
  • Track your applications in a spreadsheet so you do not lose track of where you have applied
  • Follow up if you have not heard back within 7-10 days
